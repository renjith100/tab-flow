# Your First 2 Minutes with TabFlow

You have too many tabs open. You know the one you need is somewhere in there — a GitHub PR, an article you were reading, a half-finished form — but the tab bar is a wall of tiny favicons and truncated titles. You're clicking through them one by one.

That is the problem TabFlow solves.

This tutorial walks you through a real session. By the end, you will be able to open TabFlow, find any tab in seconds, close the ones you're done with, and recover if you close the wrong one.

---

## Before you start

Make sure the TabFlow extension is installed and its icon appears in your Chrome toolbar. Then open a few tabs — any websites will do. The more you have open, the more satisfying the carousel looks.

---

## Step 1: Open TabFlow

Press **`Cmd+Shift+.`** on Mac, or **`Ctrl+Shift+.`** on Windows and Linux.

TabFlow opens as a new tab. Your browser tabs are now arranged in a 3D carousel — the tab you were just on sits in the centre, with the rest fanning out on both sides.

This is the view you will use for everything. Take a moment to look at it. Each card shows the site's favicon, the page title, and the domain name. Cards further from the centre appear smaller and angled, giving you a depth cue for how many tabs are on each side.

> **Tip:** If you want a different shortcut, you can change it any time at `chrome://extensions/shortcuts`.

---

## Step 2: Navigate the carousel

Press `→` once.

The cards slide. The tab that was to the right of centre is now in the centre. The previous centre tab has moved left and shrunk slightly.

Press `←` to move back. Press `→` a few more times to keep moving right.

You are browsing your tabs. No mouse needed — just arrow keys.

You can also use a **two-finger horizontal swipe** on a trackpad if you prefer.

---

## Step 3: Open a tab

Move through the carousel until a tab you want is in the centre.

Press `Enter`.

The centre card flashes briefly, then Chrome switches you to that tab. TabFlow closes automatically. You are now looking at the tab you chose.

That is the core loop: open TabFlow, navigate to a tab, press Enter, done.

---

## Step 4: Find a tab by searching

Open TabFlow again (`Cmd+Shift+.` or `Ctrl+Shift+.`).

This time, instead of navigating card by card, press `/`.

The search bar appears at the top. Start typing any part of a page title or domain name — for example, `GitHub` or `docs`. The carousel instantly filters to show only the matching tabs.

When you see the tab you want in the centre, press `Enter` to open it. If you want to cancel the search and go back to the full carousel, press `Esc`.

Searching is the fastest way to navigate when you have many tabs open. It takes one keystroke to start and one to open — faster than scanning a tab bar.

---

## Step 5: Close a tab you no longer need

Open TabFlow and navigate to a tab you are finished with.

Press `Esc`.

The card lifts off the carousel and arcs out of view. The tab is closed. The remaining cards slide smoothly to fill the gap.

**Drag to close:** If you prefer the mouse, you can drag any card upward or to the side. As you drag further, the card glows red. Release it when it glows red, and it closes with a small poof animation. Drag less than that distance and release — the card snaps back, nothing happens.

---

## Step 6: Undo an accidental close

Close a tab using `Esc`. Now imagine it was the wrong one.

Press `Cmd+Z` (Mac) or `Ctrl+Z` (Windows/Linux).

The tab reopens. A toast notification also appears at the bottom of the carousel with an **Undo** button — you can click that instead if you prefer.

The undo is available for as long as the toast is visible (about 4 seconds). One `Cmd+Z` restores the most recently closed tab.

---

## Step 7: Browse a tab group

If you use Chrome's tab groups, TabFlow handles them as a single card — you will see the group name and a small cluster of favicons for the tabs inside.

Navigate to a group card and press `↓` (or `Enter`).

The carousel now shows only the tabs inside that group. You can navigate, search, and open tabs exactly as before. Press `↑` to return to the main carousel.

If you don't use tab groups, skip this — it will not affect anything.

---

## You are ready

You now know everything you need to use TabFlow every day:

- `Cmd+Shift+.` — open TabFlow from anywhere
- `←` `→` — browse the carousel
- `Enter` — open the tab in the centre
- `/` — search by title or domain
- `Esc` — close the active tab
- `Cmd+Z` — undo a close

---

## Keyboard shortcuts — quick reference

| Key                            | What it does              |
| ------------------------------ | ------------------------- |
| `Cmd+Shift+.` / `Ctrl+Shift+.` | Open TabFlow              |
| `←` `→`                        | Move through the carousel |
| `Enter`                        | Open the selected tab     |
| `↓`                            | Look inside a tab group   |
| `↑`                            | Go back from a tab group  |
| `Esc`                          | Close the selected tab    |
| `/`                            | Search your tabs          |
| `Cmd+Z` / `Ctrl+Z`             | Undo the last close       |
